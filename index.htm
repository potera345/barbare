House Valarys · Origins

The Origin

House Valarys was not born from war. It was not given land or title by a king. It started with one woman and a piece of land that nobody else wanted.

Her name was Vaerys. She found the land by the sea after a fire had destroyed everything on it. Most people walked past it. She stopped.

She built a small house there. Then a bigger one. Then walls. It took her twenty years. She did it mostly alone.

People thought she was strange. A woman building a house on burned ground, talking to the sea like it could talk back. But the land was hers, the walls were solid, and slowly, people came. Not because she asked them to. Because the place felt safe.

That was the first thing House Valarys was known for. Not power. Not money. Safety.

Vaerys had one rule: anyone who came to the gates hungry would eat. Anyone who came scared would be protected. It sounds simple. In Westeros, it was rare.

By the time she died, House Valarys had a name, a small army, and a library that she had filled herself, one book at a time.

She never had a dragon. She never sat on a throne. But every person who came after her grew up knowing her story.

It was Vaerys who taught them that a house is not made of stone. It is made of the people inside it.

House Valarys · The Story

The Road to the Crown

I Part I · The Girl With Too Many Books

Barbare was eight years old when she read her first book about dragons.

It was sitting on a shelf in the library, spine cracked from years of no one touching it. She pulled it down, took it to her corner, and read it in one night with a candle that was too small for the job.

By morning her eyes hurt and she didn't care at all.

II

She was not only one thing.

She could be loud in the right rooms and quiet in the wrong ones. She laughed easily, asked too many questions, and had opinions about everything. But she also knew how to listen. Really listen, the kind where you remember what people say weeks later.

She liked people. She just liked books too.

III

Her favorite place in the whole castle was the library.

It was cold and dusty and nobody else went there. That was exactly why she liked it. She had her own corner, her own chair, her own small pile of books that she rotated like a garden.

She called it her kingdom. She told no one.

IV

One day she found a book with no title on the cover.

She opened it and found maps. Old ones, drawn by hand, of places she had never heard of. Cities with strange names. Roads that went nowhere on any other map she had seen.

She spent three weeks trying to figure out where they led. She never fully solved it. But she never stopped trying either.

V Part II · The Dragon

She was eleven when she first saw Vhaera.

The dragon was kept in the lower courtyard, away from everything else. She was young too, small enough that people thought she was harmless.

People were wrong about a lot of things.

VI

Everyone told Barbare to stay away.

She stayed away for two days. That was her limit.

On the third morning she got up before sunrise, walked down to the courtyard alone, and stood at the gate. Vhaera was already awake, sitting very still, watching the door like she had been expecting someone.

VII

They looked at each other for a long time.

Barbare didn't speak. She had read that dragons respond better to silence than to noise. She stood still and let Vhaera decide.

Vhaera walked forward slowly, stopped a few feet away, and lowered her head. Barbare reached out her hand.

VIII

After that, she came every morning.

She brought no food, made no commands, asked nothing. She just came and sat near Vhaera and sometimes read out loud from whatever book she had with her.

She liked to think Vhaera enjoyed the stories. She had no proof. But the dragon always seemed to listen.

IX Part III · The Day Everything Changed

She was fourteen when the men came to talk about the future.

They sat in the great hall with serious faces and long words and talked for hours about who would rule and how and why. Barbare sat in the back, quiet, unnoticed.

She heard everything.

X

Nobody mentioned her name once.

Not once in four hours.

She walked back to the library afterward and sat in her chair for a long time without opening a book. That almost never happened.

XI

That night she made a list.

Everything she knew. Everything she had read. Every map, every name, every story. She wrote it all down in small careful letters until the candle burned out.

At the top she wrote one question: What does a queen actually need? By the time she was done she had an answer.

XII

She started the next morning.

Not with swords or speeches. With people. She started talking to everyone in the castle the cooks, the guards, the old women who cleaned the floors at night.

She asked them what they needed. She listened. She remembered. She was fourteen years old and she was already ruling. Nobody had told her to. Nobody had stopped her either.

XIII Part IV · Before the Crown

The crown came three years later.

She was seventeen. The men who had sat in the great hall came back with different faces and the same long words, but this time her name was at the top of every list.

She accepted without smiling. Not because she wasn't happy. Because she understood, finally, what it meant.

XIV

The night before the ceremony she went to Vhaera.

She didn't say much. She sat down next to her dragon in the dark and leaned against her side and just stayed there for a while.

Vhaera made a low sound, soft, like she was trying to say something without words. Barbare understood anyway.

XV

In the morning she put on the crown.

She looked at herself in the mirror for exactly three seconds. Then she turned away and walked toward the door.

There was a lot of work to do. There always would be.

And she was ready.

House Valarys · The Conquest

The Seven Crowns

Barbare was 17 when she became Queen of House Valarys. She was 19 when she became queen of everything else.

Those two years were not quiet.

The first year she spent learning. Not from books this time. She already knew the books. She learned people. She traveled to every corner of her own lands, talked to farmers, soldiers, merchants. She wanted to know what they needed before she asked anything of them.

The second year, the other kingdoms started to notice.

Three houses came on their own. They had heard about the girl who walked her lands alone, who freed people without burning cities, who had a dragon that had never been used as a weapon. They came because they were tired of lords who only took. Barbare listened to each of them for a full day before she gave an answer.

Two more kingdoms needed convincing. She met their lords alone, no guards, no Vhaera. Nobody knows what she said. Both agreed by morning.

The last two kingdoms chose war. One changed its mind after four days. The final one fought. It was one battle. Vhaera flew for the first time in war, not over people, not over cities. Only over the field, only once.

It was enough.

At 19, Barbare Valarys stood in the great hall with seven kingdoms behind her. She did not smile. She understood what it meant.

She had work to do.
